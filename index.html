A comprehensive framework of essential human skills organized into three foundational pillars to thrive in an increasingly AI-driven world.

About the Structural Skills Framework

As artificial intelligence transforms our world, certain distinctly human capabilities become increasingly valuable. The Structural Skills Framework identifies and organizes these essential skills that will help individuals thrive alongside AI technologies.

Thinking & Problem Solving

The first pillar focuses on cognitive abilities that allow humans to navigate complexity, make sound judgments, and innovate in ways AI cannot replicate. These skills include critical thinking, creativity, adaptability, and more.

Communication & Collaboration

The second pillar encompasses skills needed to effectively interact with others, build relationships, and work together. As AI handles more routine tasks, human connection and collaboration become even more valuable.

Character & Emotional Strength

The third pillar addresses the personal qualities and emotional resilience that underpin effective interaction and sustained effort. These character traits distinguish human contributions from AI capabilities.

Curiosity

A strong desire to learn or know something; an inquisitive mindset that drives exploration and understanding.

Thinking & Problem Solving

Development Path

Learn

Read widely across different subjects, ask "why" and "how" questions frequently, follow your interests down rabbit holes.

Build

Engage in new experiences, visit museums or new places, take online courses on diverse topics, subscribe to newsletters that challenge your perspectives.

Train

Set aside dedicated time for undirected learning or exploration each week.

Mastery

Continuously seek out novel information and experiences, connect seemingly unrelated ideas, become a lifelong learner.

How Curiosity Is Applied
In the Workplace

A UX researcher who consistently asks 'why' questions during user interviews, uncovering deeper needs that competitors miss, resulting in more innovative product features.

For Leadership

A team leader who encourages staff to spend 10% of their time exploring new technologies, creating a culture where continuous learning drives innovation.

Personal Development

Taking a different route home each week, trying new cuisines monthly, or subscribing to a magazine outside your usual interests to expand your worldview.
